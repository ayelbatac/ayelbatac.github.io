I am an epidemiologist, social and behavioural health scientist, and health economist in training, dedicated to generating research that informs public health policy, advances health equity, and supports evidence-based decision-making.

I am a second-year Master of Science student in Public Health (Health Economics) at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, with over six years of experience spanning research, data analytics, and applied public health practice.

My work addresses a range of urgent and evolving challenges in global health. I am currently contributing to the evaluation of behavioural interventions to increase sexually transmitted infection (STI) testing among men who have sex with men in China, and leading international research on anaphylaxis treatment access and emergency care readiness. I am also evaluating community-wide tuberculosis screening strategies in remote Arctic communities in Canada. I recently developed outbreak debrief guidance and clinical tools for mpox, supporting national efforts to strengthen public health communication and frontline clinical response during a rapidly changing epidemic.

My earlier research focused on allergy, immunology, and vaccine safety. I contributed to evidence synthesis on COVID-19 vaccine hesitancy and worked to identify risk factors for vaccine-induced allergic reactions, with the goal of supporting safe and inclusive immunization strategies. I also examined the impact of atopic dermatitis on the maternal-infant bond, reflecting an early interest in the psychosocial dimensions of chronic disease and the broader context of caregiving and health systems.

My research draws on both quantitative and qualitative methods, with a particular emphasis on integrating insights from epidemiology and biostatistics; health systems research; implementation science; and the social and behavioural sciences. I take a mixed methods approach not only as a research strategy, but as a reflection of the complexity of public health inquiry. These challenges rarely lend themselves to a single dataset or analytic method. Instead, they often involve a series of interrelated considerations that require diverse forms of data and complementary methodological approaches. By combining statistical analysis, qualitative inquiry, and systems thinking, I aim to build a more complete understanding of how health systems function—and how they can be strengthened. My work is grounded in an interdisciplinary perspective, with a strong emphasis on equity and real-world impact.

I have collaborated with researchers from Canada, the United States, the United Kingdom, Finland, Sweden, Italy, China, Japan, and Australia, contributing to work that spans disciplines, health systems, and regional contexts. I have published peer-reviewed articles in leading journals across allergy and clinical immunology, focusing on health system readiness, access to emergency care, and the application of evidence in real-world settings.

At the core of my work is a commitment to making research matter beyond academic settings. I strive to ensure that evidence is both accessible and actionable for those involved in healthcare delivery, policy development, and frontline implementation. I am especially focused on translating knowledge into practice in ways that reach the populations it is intended to serve—particularly those with the greatest need—and that drive meaningful, measurable, and lasting improvements in health outcomes. Ultimately, I aim to bridge the gap between academic research and its real-world use in public health decision-making.

Professional Experience

Data Scientist and Scientific Lead 2024 – 2025

Department of Education and Early Childhood Learning, Government of Manitoba, Winnipeg, Canada

  • Provincial child care demand forecasting: Applied predictive analytics and economic modelling using supervised machine learning to forecast demand for early learning and child care services across Manitoba, Canada, supporting data-driven decision-making, resource optimization, and strategic policy planning.
  • Spatiotemporal analyses of Manitoba child care facilities: Conducted spatial and temporal analyses of child care facilities in Manitoba, Canada, examining access patterns, service distribution, and economic disparities over time and across regions to identify inequities and inform evidence-based planning and resource allocation.
  • Geospatial mapping of child care service gaps: Conducted geospatial analyses to identify and map child care deserts—areas with limited or no access to licensed child care—highlighting critical service gaps and generating actionable insights to inform strategic policy development and guide resource allocation.
  • Enhancing institutional data and information governance: Developed and implemented comprehensive data governance frameworks to improve data quality, accessibility, and infrastructure, resulting in policies addressing key areas such as data quality assurance, security, privacy, lifecycle management, access and usage, and ethical data use.

Research Assistant 2020 – 2024

Children’s Hospital Research Institute of Manitoba, Winnipeg, Canada

  • Psychosocial and financial burdens of pediatric food allergy on caregivers: Analyzed quantitative and qualitative literature to examine the emotional, social, and economic challenges faced by caregivers of children with food allergies, with particular attention to the heightened burdens linked to severe allergies, multiple allergens, and comorbid conditions.
  • Psychological and social impacts of pediatric food allergy: Analyzed quantitative and qualitative data to assess the burden of pediatric food allergy, addressing gaps in health-related quality of life and psychosocial well-being, and generating insights to inform the development of targeted health services and support strategies for children and adolescents.
  • Impact of infantile atopic dermatitis on maternal-infant bonding: Used a mixed-methods approach to examine how the burdens of treating infantile atopic dermatitis and associated stress affect maternal-infant emotional attachment, addressing a key research gap and contributing evidence to support early intervention strategies and caregiver-centred clinical guidance.
  • Evaluation of instruments measuring household-level financial burden of food allergy: Assessed existing questionnaires evaluating the economic impact of immunoglobulin E (IgE)-mediated food allergy at the household level, identifying key limitations including response burden, insufficient attention to economic precarity, and limited relevance to emerging social determinants such as food insecurity. Findings informed the design of a more targeted, streamlined tool to better capture the financial realities faced by affected families.

Research Assistant 2020 – 2024

Department of Pediatrics and Child Health, Max Rady College of Medicine, Rady Faculty of Health Sciences, University of Manitoba, Winnipeg, Canada

  • Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) vaccine hesitancy in individuals with allergic disease: Conducted qualitative research to explore attitudes, fears, and decision-making factors driving vaccine hesitancy among people with allergic disease, highlighted the role of misinformation and concerns about allergic reactions despite their low incidence, and provided evidence that informed knowledge translation efforts to communicate accurate vaccine risks and reduce public fears.
  • Potential factors associated with COVID-19 vaccine-induced allergic reactions: Investigated and analyzed contributors to allergic reactions following COVID-19 vaccination, addressed misconceptions about polyethylene glycol and related excipients, and delivered evidence-based recommendations to enhance risk assessment, clinical management, and vaccine confidence among individuals with allergies.
  • Incidence of COVID-19 vaccine-induced anaphylaxis: Assessed anaphylaxis incidence following COVID-19 vaccination, analyzing variations across vaccine brands and types, identifying risk factors such as allergic disease history, and addressing misinformation to promote vaccine confidence, inform public health guidelines, and enhance pandemic preparedness through improved dissemination of accurate vaccine safety information.
  • Detection and prevention of online recruitment fraud in digital health research: Designed a framework to identify and mitigate fraud in virtual participant recruitment for qualitative studies by addressing misrepresentation and verifying authenticity through metadata analysis, rigorous screening, and digital consent, delivering actionable guidelines to enhance data integrity and safeguard research validity.

Epidemiologist May 2021 – Aug 2022

Department of Health, Seniors and Long-Term Care, Government of Manitoba, Winnipeg, Canada

  • Epidemiology of severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) variants in paediatric and educational settings: Conducted an epidemiological investigation of transmission dynamics, secondary attack rates, and population impact of the Alpha (B.1.1.7) and Delta (B.1.617.2) variants among children, schools, and early learning and child care facilities in Manitoba, Canada, providing critical data to refine outbreak response strategies, optimize school safety protocols, and guide vaccination prioritization in educational environments.
  • COVID-19 outbreaks in healthcare settings: Conducted epidemiological analyses of outbreak dynamics, risk factors, secondary attack rates, and outcomes of COVID-19 across healthcare environments—including assisted living facilities, long-term care homes, hospitals, health centres, and personal care homes—in Manitoba, Canada, providing evidence to guide targeted containment measures, optimize resource allocation, and inform outbreak mitigation strategies.
  • COVID-19 epidemiology in Northern, Indigenous, and rural communities: Analyzed COVID-19 incidence, prevalence, mortality rates, and associated health disparities in Northern, Indigenous, and rural communities in Manitoba, Canada, providing epidemiological evidence to inform equity-driven public health responses and culturally responsive interventions tailored to the unique needs of these populations.
  • Severe outcomes associated with COVID-19: Conducted surveillance and analysis of hospitalizations, intensive care unit admissions, and deaths in Manitoba, Canada, identifying key risk factors and driving data-informed decisions on healthcare resource allocation and public health interventions.
